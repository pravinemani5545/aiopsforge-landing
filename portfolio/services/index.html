questions

The ones I actually get.

If yours isn't here, ask on the call.

What stack do you build on?

Claude Code + Vercel (serverless + cron + KV) + Anthropic API. I'll work in your stack if it matches — TypeScript, Next.js, Supabase, Stripe, Beehiiv, Whop are all comfortable. I won't build on n8n, Make, or Zapier — fragile, expensive at scale, and you can't read the code six months from now.

Do you sign NDAs?

Yes — happy to. Standard mutual NDA at the scoping call stage. For audits where I'm interviewing your team, I sign before any conversation happens.

Who owns the code?

You do, outright. All deliverables are work-for-hire under a written agreement. You get the full source, the deployment, and the right to modify or sell. I keep no claim. (I do learn from the work — that's the trade-off of working with a solo operator who's also building in public.)

Why fixed price instead of hourly?

Hourly aligns incentives badly — I'd be incentivized to take longer, you'd be incentivized to micro-manage. Fixed-price aligns us on outcome: we both want the thing done well, fast, and right the first time.

What if I'm in a different timezone?

I'm in Toronto (ET). Async-default works for most engagements — Slack/email + recorded Loom updates. I'll do live calls in your timezone within 9am–9pm ET; outside that, written-first.

Do you do equity-for-work?

No. I've watched too many friends regret it. Cash, fair price, no equity entanglement.

Can you start next week?

Usually yes for sprints, sometimes for custom builds. Audits typically book 2–3 weeks out. Always ask — calendars change.

What if the scope changes mid-project?

For sprints: scope is fixed, period. For audits and custom builds: anything outside the written spec is a separate quote. I'll tell you the impact before you decide, never silently re-scope.
