how it goes

From inquiry to shipped, four steps.

No "discovery deck," no 60-min kickoff calls just to align timezones. Lean, written-first, async-defaulted. Full breakdown at /services.

STEP 01

You book the call.

30 min · within 48 hrs. 4 questions on the booking form: what's broken, who you are, when you need it, rough budget. I read everything personally.

STEP 02

We scope live.

Operator-to-operator call. Sharpen the scope, agree on service tier, identify fit-killers early. No pitch deck. If it isn't a fit, I'll point you to who is.

STEP 03

Written quote + contract.

Same day. Fixed price, payment terms, milestones. You sign with a Stripe link. Work starts when you say go — usually within a week of signing.

STEP 04

I ship · you review.

Daily progress notes. Milestone demos via Loom. Final handover with README, deployment guide, and a Loom walkthrough you can revisit.

objections, handled

Likely questions, short answers.

Common ones up top. Detailed answers (and 6 more) live on /services.

Why don't you list prices on this page?
Every project has different signal. The 30-min call gives me 90% of what I need to scope; you walk out with a written quote same-day. Listing prices anchors decisions before context exists.
01 / 10
Do you really not use n8n or Make?
No. Vercel cron + serverless functions versioned in git is the same primitive with zero platform fee, full observability, and no vendor lock-in. Cheatsheet here.
02 / 10
What does "solo by choice" mean for delivery risk?
One person, fixed-scope contracts, no subcontracting. If I get hit by a bus, you get a refund clause + escrow. The flip-side: zero handoff friction, no PM tax, decisions in hours not weeks.
03 / 10
Can you work in our existing stack instead of yours?
Yes, with a caveat: if your stack is the actual problem, I'll say so on the call. Otherwise I work in Next.js, Supabase, Django, Rails, Laravel, FastAPI — whatever the codebase wants.
