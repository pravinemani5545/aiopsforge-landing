your first hour

Four steps, in this order.

Two are written end-to-end below. Two more are queued — outlines visible so you know what's coming.

01
READY · 10 MIN
READ FIRST10 MIN

Read the orientation — what AIOpsForge is and isn't.

The four pillars (weekly build guides · resources · tools · community mentoring), how membership works, what to expect in your first week. If you came expecting a content library or a Discord hangout, this is where you find out you're in the wrong place.

Read orientation →Skip if returning
02
READY · 15 MIN
SETUP15 MIN

Set up Claude Code locally — the AOF default stack.

Install Claude Code, configure your ~/.claude/CLAUDE.md, add the AOF skills bundle, and verify /init works. Other tools (Cursor, v0) are fine — but every weekly guide assumes you have Claude Code working as a baseline.

Setup walkthrough →Claude Code cheatsheet
03
DRAFTING · 20 MIN
DRAFTING20 MIN

Pick a first build — what will you ship this week?

A 5-question intake (need-planning tool) that turns a vague AI idea into a scoped, shippable build. By the end of step 3, you'll have a specific build picked, the right weekly guide queued, and a ship-by date on the calendar.

Open intake →Browse weekly guides
04
SOON · 15 MIN
SOON15 MIN

Ship + share — your first build on the community board.

After completing the build, drop a short post in the community thread for week N: what you built, what broke, what you'd do differently. This is how community mentoring works — your wins become tomorrow's tutorials, your stuck moments become someone else's "I had that exact problem."
